Environment and Lighting:

Designed a vibrant stage environment with animated lights to match the energy of the dance.

Concept Development:

Selected regional Mexican dances such as zapateado, wepa, and cumbia for their expressive and rhythmic qualities.
Focused on solo dances due to mo-cap logistics and time constraints.

Why It Matters:

To animate traditional Mexican dances authentically and preserve cultural expression through motion capture technology.

This project explores how advanced animation techniques can preserve and celebrate cultural heritage while pushing creative boundaries.

This project brings traditional Mexican dance to life through motion capture technology. Using mo-cap data and 3D animation tools, I captured the elegance of regional Mexican dance styles, blending cultural expression with cutting-edge technology.

Animation Refinement:

Imported raw mo-cap data into Maya for rigging and refinement.
Adjusted character movements to address mo-cap imperfections, such as floating or clipping.

Motion Capture Setup:

Utilized a mo-cap studio to record dance movements from a performer.
Dealt with challenges such as foot floating and syncing motion accurately to enhance animation realism.
