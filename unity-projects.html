Approach and Process:

Asylum DXR is a psychological horror virtual reality experience designed to immerse players in a chilling, interactive environment where their sanity is constantly tested. Built using Unity and XR technologies, the game features dynamic gameplay elements, a sanity system, and an engaging storyline to enhance the player's experience.

To create a fully immersive and interactive VR environment that challenges the player's perception and decision-making.
To explore the intersection of storytelling, game mechanics, and VR design to evoke psychological tension and engagement.

Technical Implementation:

Built using Unity with the XR Interaction Toolkit for VR mechanics.
Developed custom scripts to handle sanity levels, player interactions, and environmental effects.

Game Mechanics:

Implemented a sanity system where the player's mental state deteriorates over time, affecting vision, triggering unsettling audio cues, and influencing interactions.
Introduced dynamic elements like a toxic gas system to drive urgency and limit backtracking.

Interactive Gameplay:
Players solve puzzles and interact with objects to progress through the asylum.

Environmental Design:
Atmospheric lighting and textures to evoke a sense of unease.
Carefully crafted environmental storytelling through props, notes, and visual cues.
